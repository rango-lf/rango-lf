ABOUT ME.

Most people call me Rango, but other variations of my name include "Commander", Django", "Tango", and "Rambo". I prefer masculine terminology (he/him) when referring to me.

I was originally born in Richmond; a small city that is part of the Greater Vancouver Area. I was above average in high school but quite unhappy most of the time. Both my father (civil) and older sister (eng. physics) are engineers from UBC, so I chose to attend UofT out of spite.

Driven by my intense high school hyperfixation on Chernobyl, I'm passionate about nuclear engineering--particularly the development of Gen III+ and IV nuclear reactors. I intend to complete a BASc in mechanical engineering with a specialisation in energy systems and mechatronics, while gaining some practical experience with nuclear energy in the process. After this adventure, I'd like to pursue a MASc in nuclear reactor physics, and if I survive that, then a PhD in engineering education.

In real life, I unfortunately do not look exactly as pictured above. If you see a nerdy Asian guy with a stupid haircut, a Lenovo Yoga laptop, and a copy of Homer's The Iliad--that's probably me. I can usually be found in the MIE common room, located in the Mining Building, when I'm not in class or busy with something else. Don't be afraid to talk to me!
